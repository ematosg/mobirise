Marqués del Riscal Wines

Turning an old wine into an avant garde experience

Marqués de Riscal Wines had a perception problem: It looks old and very expensive. And for a burgeoning market of Dominican wine drinkers that makes it unappealing, especially when compared with some of the tastefully designed labels of some entry level wines.

Our Solution:
After researching both the history of the winery and the reality of the market, we decided to compare it to its motherland; Spain, a land that is both very traditional and very modern. House of great modern designers and architects and at the same time the oldest restaurant in the world and some of the oldest cave paintings.

We created ads that reflected the modernism of Spain bottled inside Marques de Riscal. Together with this, a series of art and design events hosted by the brand that helped solidify the brand and its new claim: “Like Spain, traditionally avant grade”

Orange

Reinforcing brand loyalty on a small budget

Our Client, Orange Telecom, came to us with a problem: Internal polls showed that the administrative employees didn't feel as part of the company and it was affecting performance. They wanted to create an internal campaign to boost brand loyalty, but they didn't have a budget allocated for this.

Our Solution:
What originally was planned just for for the Administrative branch  we decided to refocus into a bigger effort, so we interviewed employees from every single department in the company during small breaks created by their supervisor . Together with the employees input, we figured out a way to explain in simple words and actions what their position was and how that would translate into their passion for what they do. The we took several pictures and proceeded to make a sketch to flesh out the idea.

Not only this was a resounding success, it used as a best practice for internal communications for Orange worldwide.

UNICEF's alguien.do

Helping the youth of the Dominican Republic understand who they are.

Alguien (Spanish for someone/somebody) is UNICEF's way of helping the youth of the Dominican Republic understand and accept themselves while also learn how to protect themselves from predators and any form of abuse both on and offline.

Prior the launch of the website, we designed a campaign with examples of self acceptance (using the prhase "I am somebody") and a follow up dealing with abuse and neglect (with the prhase "No mather how you feel, you are somebody") that coincided with the the launch.

Together with these campaigns, a series of experiential marketing actions were used in schools, malls and cinemas to spread the message and get the target audience to visit the site.
